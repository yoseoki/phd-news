# CS Research Seminar 2026

## Who Needs to Attend

- **All PhD students** taking Cross-Disciplinary Seminar in Computer Science [0BLD514]
- **TWINS registration is mandatory**
- *Exceptions:* If you can't attend for valid reasons (full-time job, research stay elsewhere), consult your supervisor — you may take an alternative course instead

## Your Three Tasks

1. **Give one presentation** on your research (submit the rehearsal recording one week in advance)
2. **Attend other students' presentations** (~10 weeks) and participate in discussions — some students will be assigned to pre-watch recordings and give comments
3. **Submit one lightweight report**

## Your Presentation — Requirements

- **Format:** Onsite (hybrid); online attendance only allowed with good reason
- **Length:** 10–15 minutes
- **Prepare:** Slides + a 10-minute rehearsal recording
- **Deadline:** Upload both **at least one week before** your presentation date

**Expected content:**
- Self-introduction
- The "big picture": gentle intro to your field, your motivation, why it's challenging, and your ideas (don't go too deep into details)

⚠️ **Important:** This is **not a thesis defense**. The audience are not experts in your topic — don't just reuse your master's defense or conference slides. Share motivation and limitations rather than trying to impress.

## Discussion Etiquette

- Ask questions — if something is unclear, it's the speaker's fault, not yours
- Keep questions short, max one per talk
- Respect the speaker and other attendees
- Prof. Millar may give comments at the end

- **2nd session:** May 11 (Mon.) — student talks begin
- ~10 sessions total, depending on enrollment
